REEL FOUR — THE WORK

Ministry & Business

MINISTRY

Tribe of Issachar Church

Named for the tribe known for understanding the times — a fitting name for a man who always seems to know exactly when someone needs to hear "you are not alone."

Here, sermons are personal letters disguised as Sunday messages. People don't just attend — they're checked on, by name, by phone, by 7am text.

BUSINESS

The Hills Foundation

Built not to chase margins but to fund mercy. Every contract signed was, in his mind, just another way to keep a promise made to his family years earlier: that they would never go without.

The foundation now extends that same promise outward — to families who aren't his, but who he's decided to provide for anyway.

A LETTER FROM T.J

Dad,

I don't think I ever told you that I noticed. I noticed the early mornings, the calls you took outside so we wouldn't hear the worry in your voice, the way you'd still ask "are you alright?" even on the days you clearly weren't.

You built a church. You built a company. People will write about The Hills Foundation and Tribe of Issachar, and they should — those things mattered. But none of it is the thing I'll tell my own children about.

I'll tell them about a man who said "it's my job to provide na" like it was the simplest fact in the world, and then quietly made it true for thirty years straight. I'll tell them about a father who apologized first, even when he didn't have to, because being right always mattered less to him than being good.

Your greatest work was never on a stage or in a contract. It was us. It was Mum's laugh staying easy after all these years. It was Joy, Joshua, Israel, and me — turning out alright, because you kept asking if we were.

Happy Father's Day. Are you alright? I hope, for once, you let yourself say no, and let someone else carry it.

With everything,
T.J

The church was built.

The businesses were built.

But your greatest work was the family you raised.

OLUWAFEMI PAUL ABATI
